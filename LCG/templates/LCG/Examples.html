Here are some examples, please read them carefully. If you don't understand something here, be sure to ask the experimenter who will be able to help you.

  1. Non-matching numbers
    Player 1 rolls a 5, Player 2 rolls a 1. Both players report their numbers accurately to the leader. Because their numbers do not match, the leader does not need to make a decision, and all players get 0 points this round. This refers to outcome 1 in the graphic (see below).

  2. Accurate report of matching numbers
    Player 1 rolls a 6, Player 2 rolls a 6. Both players report their numbers accurately to the leader. Because they report matching numbers, the leader faces the decision whether to rely on their report, or whether to verify it.

    Let's say the leader decides to verify. Verifying comes at a cost, however: even though the numbers were accurately reported, all players only get half the amount of points this round (i.e. 6 / 2 = 3 points). This refers to scenario 2 in the graphic.

    Note: if the leader chose to rely here, all players would have gotten 6 points this round. This refers to scenario 3 in the graphic.

  3. Inaccurate report of matching numbers
    Player 1 rolls a 5, Player 2 rolls a 3. Player 1 reports the rolled number accurately, but Player 2 chooses to also report a 5. Because the numbers match, the leader again faces the decision to rely or verify.

    This time, the leader decides to rely. Even though this report is inaccurate, this means that in {{ Constants.LoseGameNegPercent }}% of the times, all players will receive 5 points. However, in {{ Constants.LoseGamePercent }}% of the times, the group will lose the game, meaning that all players will stop playing, and lose all their earnings they have made so far. Thus, if they so far had earned 50 points, they will end the game with 0 points. This refers to scenario 5 in the graphic.

    If the leader had chosen to verify, the lie would have been discovered, and all players would have received 0 points this round. However, because the lie was discovered, there would have been no chance of losing the game, and the group would continue to play the game, keeping their previous profits. This refers to scenario 4 in the graphic.
