As introduced, this study involves two separate games. Now, you will begin with the first game, the "Rely or Verify" game!

In this game, you will play together with two other players that are randomly chosen from the group of participants in this session. Your identity and that of your co-players will remain anonymous.

This game is a multiplayer dice roll game with three people, all of which take on different roles in the game.
Players 1 and 2 will form a team. Player 3 will be the leader of the group.
You are {{ player.role }}.

The game has the following structure:

  1. Player 1 rolls a die and reports his / her outcome.
  2. Player 2 sees what Player 1 has reported. Then, Player 2 rolls a die and reports their outcome.
  3. If the reported numbers don't match, nothing happens, and there is no payoff this round.
  4. If the reported numbers match, the leader can decide whether to rely on the group's report, or to verify it.
  5. Depending on the leader's choice, the rolled number, and whether the team's report was correct, the payoff for this round is determined (see below).
  6. The game is repeated for {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds.

Payoff Calculations

The precise payoff varies depending on team performance and leader choice (keep in mind that points will converted to kr at the end of this experiment in a ratio of 2 points = 1 kroner!).

If the reported numbers don't match, no one will receive any points this round. If the reported numbers match, there are four different possible scenarios:

  1. Leader Relies (no checking)
    1. If the team's report was correct, all players receive same amount of points as the reported number. For example, if both players reported a 5, all players will get 5 points.
    2. If the team's report was incorrect, all players receive the same amount of points as the rolled number. However, there is a {{ Constants.LoseGamePercent }} % chance your team will lose the game. Losing the game means that you will lose all earnings you have made so far, and the game will end.

  2. Leader Verifies (checks results)
    1. If the team's report was correct, all players receive half the amount of points as the reported number. For example, if both players reported a 5, all players will get 2.5 points.
    2. If the team's report was incorrect, all players receive 0 points this round.
