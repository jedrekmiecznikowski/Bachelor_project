Consent form


1. Title of research

You are being invited to take part in the research study Decision Making task. We would like to ask you for your consent to participate in the study and for us to treat your data in agreement with data protection legislation. Before you decide to participate in this study, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take the time to read the following information carefully. Please ask the researcher if there is anything that is not clear or if you need more information.


2. Project description and aim of the study

How different groups allocate resources in a decision-making task.

3. Data controller, research group and principal investigator

Principal investigator: Jedrzej Miecznikowski, 201811497@post.au.dk

Data controller: Jedrzej Miecznikowski, 201811497@post.au.dk

Other researchers: Oana Vuculescu, oanav@mgmt.au.dk


4. Study procedure

First you will be asked to fill out a short survey. Then read the instructions and play a game based on decision-making with others. The game will consist of 25 rounds in which you will be asked to make a decision about how you want to invest a certain amount of experimental currency which is interchangeable for DKK. Each participantâ€™s decisions will affect not only their own earnings, but also the earnings of the whole group. After the task, you will be asked a few follow-up questions. The whole procedure will take up to an hour. 

5. Benefits and risks

There are no risks beyond those encountered in normal everyday life associated with this study. 

Your expected earnings for participating in the study are based on your performance. Depending on your decision making you can get from 40 DKK to 170DKK. The study is expected to last 45-60 minutes. 


6. Type of personal data and when it is deleted/anonymised 

We process normal personal information in form of your CPR-number to make payments for participation in the study. The CPR-numbers are connected to data from the research only for the purposes of payment based on performance and are deleted as soon as payments are finalized. The data will be deleted on 2nd of May at the latest and up to that point it will only be stored on an AU database and handled by AU employees; no external parties will have access to it. 

7. Withdrawal of consent

Participation is voluntary and you may withdraw your consent at any time without stating a reason.  This is done by emailing Jedrzej at 201811497@post.au.dk, however be wary the data is completely anonymised after the payment is made. Therefore, one can withdraw only before the payment is made. Furthermore, all data will be anonymized by 2nd of May. After that point the data cannot be withdrawn because individuals can no longer be identified in the data.
